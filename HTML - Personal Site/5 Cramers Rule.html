Topics on this page

Under certain circumstances, it is possible to use determinants to solve a series of linear equations.

\begin{bmatrix} a{_1}{_,}{_1} & a{_1}{_,}{_2}\\ a{_2}{_,}{_1} & a{_2}{_,}{_2}\end{bmatrix}

Assume a system of equations:

$a{_1}{_,}{_1}x{_1} + a{_1}{_,}{_2}x{_2} = k{_1}$
$a{_2}{_,}{_1}x{_1} + a{_2}{_,}{_2}x{_2} = k{_2}$

We could write the solution to this system as:

$x{_1} = \dfrac {a{_2}{_,}{_2}k{_1} - {a{_1}{_,}{_2}k{_2}}} {a{_1}{_,}{_1}a{_2}{_,}{_2}-a{_2}{_,}{_1}a{_1}{_,}{_2}}$


$x{_2} = \dfrac {a{_1}{_,}{_1}k{_2} - {a{_2}{_,}{_2}k{_1}}} {a{_1}{_,}{_1}a{_2}{_,}{_2}-a{_2}{_,}{_1}a{_1}{_,}{_2}}$


Here, we can see the denominator of both solutions is the same: $a{_1}{_,}{_1}a{_2}{_,}{_2}-a{_2}{_,}{_1}a{_1}{_,}{_2}$. This denominator is also the same as the determinant of a 2x2 matrix - as a recap:
$det (A) \begin{bmatrix}a{_1}{_,}{_1}&a{_1}{_,}{_2}\\a{_2}{_,}{_1}&a{_2}{_,}{_2}\end{bmatrix} = \begin{vmatrix}a{_1}{_,}{_1}&a{_1}{_,}{_2}\\a{_2}{_,}{_1}&a{_2}{_,}{_2}\end{vmatrix} = a{_1}{_,}{_1} a{_2}{_,}{_2}-a{_1}{_,}{_2} a{_2}{_,}{_1}$
If this determinant is zero, then the set of equations has no unique solution.

To find the numerators:

$x{_1} = \dfrac {\huge{a{_2}{_,}{_2}k{_1} - {a{_1}{_,}{_2}k{_2}}}} {\small{a{_1}{_,}{_1}a{_2}{_,}{_2}-a{_2}{_,}{_1}a{_1}{_,}{_2}}} \searrow$ $\begin{vmatrix}k{_1}&a{_1}{_,}{_2}\\k{_2}&a{_2}{_,}{_2}\end{vmatrix}$


$x{_2} = \dfrac {\huge{a{_1}{_,}{_1}k{_2} - {a{_2}{_,}{_2}k{_1}}}} {\small{a{_1}{_,}{_1}a{_2}{_,}{_2}-a{_2}{_,}{_1}a{_1}{_,}{_2}}} \searrow \begin{vmatrix}a{_1}{_,}{_1} & k{_1}\\a{_2}{_,}{_1} & k{_2}\end{vmatrix}$

From this we can re-write the solution of a 2 x 2 system as : $$\huge{x{_1} = \dfrac {\begin{vmatrix}k{_1}&a{_1}{_,}{_2}\\k{_2}&a{_2}{_,}{_2}\end{vmatrix}} {\begin{vmatrix}a{_1}{_,}{_1}&a{_1}{_,}{_2}\\a{_2}{_,}{_1}&a{_2}{_,}{_2}\end{vmatrix}}}$$
$$\huge{x{_2} = \dfrac {\begin{vmatrix}a{_1}{_,}{_1} & k{_1}\\a{_2}{_,}{_1} & k{_2}\end{vmatrix}} {\begin{vmatrix}a{_1}{_,}{_1}&a{_1}{_,}{_2}\\a{_2}{_,}{_1}&a{_2}{_,}{_2}\end{vmatrix}}}$$

This is what is known as Cramer's Rule.
Any system of simultaneous equations:
$a{_1}{_,}{_1}x{_1} + a{_1}{_,}{_2}x{_2} + \dots + a{_1}{_,}{_n}x{_n} = k{_1}$
$a{_2}{_,}{_1}x{_1} + a{_2}{_,}{_2}x{_2} + \dots + a{_2}{_,}{_n}x{_n} = k{_2}$
$a{_n}{_1}{_,}{_1}x{_1} + a{_n}{_2}{_,}{_2}x{_2} + \dots + a{_n}{_,}{_n}x{_n}= k{_n}$

Can be written as: $\begin{pmatrix}a{_1}{_,}{_1} & a{_1}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_1}{_,}{_n}\\ a{_2}{_,}{_1} & a{_2}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_2}{_,}{_n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\a{_n}{_,}{_1} & a{_n}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_n}{_,}{_n}\end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x{_1}\\x{_2}\\ \vdots\\x{_n} \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} k{_1}\\k{_2}\\ \vdots\\k{_n} \end{pmatrix}     \large{AX = K}$

The solution of a system of simultaneous equations $\large{AX = K}$ can be obtained as a ratio of two determinants:

$x{_1} = \dfrac {\begin{vmatrix}k{_1} & a{_1}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_1}{_,}{_n}\\ k{_2} & a{_2}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_2}{_,}{_n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\k{_n} & a{_n}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_n}{_,}{_n}\end{vmatrix}} {\begin{vmatrix}a{_1}{_,}{_1} & a{_1}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_1}{_,}{_n}\\ a{_2}{_,}{_1} & a{_2}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_2}{_,}{_n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\a{_n}{_,}{_1} & a{_n}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_n}{_,}{_n}\end{vmatrix}}, x{_2} = \dfrac {\begin{vmatrix}a{_1}{_,}{_1} & k{_1} & \dots & a{_1}{_,}{_n}\\ a{_2}{_,}{_2} & k{_2} & \dots & a{_2}{_,}{_n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ a{_n}{_,}{_1} & k{_n} & \dots & a{_n}{_,}{_n}\end{vmatrix}} {\begin{vmatrix}a{_1}{_,}{_1} & a{_1}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_1}{_,}{_n}\\ a{_2}{_,}{_1} & a{_2}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_2}{_,}{_n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\a{_n}{_,}{_1} & a{_n}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_n}{_,}{_n}\end{vmatrix}}, x{_n} = \dfrac {\begin{vmatrix}a{_1}{_,}{_1} & a{_1}{_,}{_2} & \dots & k{_1}\\ a{_2}{_,}{_1} & a{_2}{_,}{_2} & \dots & k{_2} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\a{_n}{_,}{_1} & a{_n}{_,}{_2} & \dots & k{_n}\end{vmatrix}} {\begin{vmatrix}a{_1}{_,}{_1} & a{_1}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_1}{_,}{_n}\\ a{_2}{_,}{_1} & a{_2}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_2}{_,}{_n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\a{_n}{_,}{_1} & a{_n}{_,}{_2} & \dots & a{_n}{_,}{_n}\end{vmatrix}}$
