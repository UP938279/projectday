If matrices are of the same size, they can be easily added or subtracted to or from each other. This is done element by element. The final matrix will be have the same dimensions as the starting matrices.

Adding Matrices

We start with Matrix A:    $\begin{bmatrix} a{_1}{_,}{_1} & a{_1}{_,}{_2}\\ a{_2}{_,}{_1} & a{_2}{_,}{_2}\end{bmatrix}$

and Matrix B:             $\begin{bmatrix} b{_1}{_,}{_1} & b{_1}{_,}{_2}\\ b{_2}{_,}{_1} & b{_2}{_,}{_2}\end{bmatrix}$


$ A^T\pm B^T = (A \pm B)^T $

We will add element by element
$A+ B = \begin{bmatrix} a{_1}{_,}{_1} + b{_1}{_,}{_1} & a{_1}{_,}{_2} + b{_1}{_,}{_2}\\ a{_2}{_,}{_1} + b{_2}{_,}{_1}& a{_2}{_,}{_2} + b{_2}{_,}{_2}\end{bmatrix}$

To show an example using numbers:

Matrix A =    $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4\end{bmatrix}$
Matrix B =    $\begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8\end{bmatrix}$
To work out the resultant Matrix:     $\begin{bmatrix} 1 + 5 & 2 + 6 \\ 3 + 7 & 4 + 8\end{bmatrix}$

Which will give the matrix:    $\begin{bmatrix} 6 & 8 \\ 10 & 12\end{bmatrix}$




Subtracting Matrices

Again, we do this element by element.

$\begin{bmatrix} a{_1}{_,}{_1} & a{_1}{_,}{_2}\\ a{_2}{_,}{_1} & a{_2}{_,}{_2}\end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} b{_1}{_,}{_1} & b{_1}{_,}{_2}\\ b{_2}{_,}{_1} & b{_2}{_,}{_2}\end{bmatrix}$


$A - B =    \begin{bmatrix} a{_1}{_,}{_1} - b{_1}{_,}{_1} & a{_1}{_,}{_2} - b{_1}{_,}{_2}\\ a{_2}{_,}{_1} - b{_2}{_,}{_1}& a{_2}{_,}{_2} - b{_2}{_,}{_2}\end{bmatrix}$


Again, $ A^T\pm B^T = (A \pm B)^T $

And another example with numbers

Matrix $A =    \begin{bmatrix} 10 & 9 \\ 8 & 7\end{bmatrix}$

Matrix $B =    \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 6 & 8\end{bmatrix}$

Will give the matrix: $\begin{bmatrix} 8 & 5 \\ 2 & -1\end{bmatrix}$
